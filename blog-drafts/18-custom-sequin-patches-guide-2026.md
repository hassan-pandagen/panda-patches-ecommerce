## What Are Sequin Patches?

Sequin patches are made by stitching small, flat, metallic or plastic discs (sequins) onto a fabric backing in overlapping rows. Each sequin is typically 3 to 6mm in diameter and is anchored through a center hole. The overlapping arrangement creates a continuous reflective surface that shimmers as the wearer moves.

The term "sequin" comes from the Arabic word "sikka," meaning a coin or die used to stamp metal. Sequins have been used in fashion and decoration for centuries, but their application to patches and emblems has surged in the last decade, driven by the rise of streetwear, festival fashion, and social media-friendly apparel that demands visual impact.

Custom sequin patches are produced in two primary styles:

**Flat sequin patches** use a single layer of sequins in one color. The sequins are permanently fixed in one direction, creating a consistent sparkle effect. This is the simpler, more affordable option and works well for logos, text, and bold graphic designs.

**Flip (reversible) sequin patches** use two-toned sequins that are anchored at the center, allowing them to flip between two colors when brushed by hand. One side shows color A, the other shows color B. This interactive quality makes flip sequin patches popular for kids' apparel, fashion merchandise, and novelty products. The "reveal" effect (brush to change the image) is a major selling point at retail.

## Types of Sequin Patches

### Flat Sequin Patches

Flat sequins are the standard option. Each sequin is fixed in position and reflects light in a single direction. The result is a consistent, shimmering surface.

**Best for:** Fashion branding, stage costumes, dance team uniforms, evening wear accents, and any application where consistent sparkle matters.

**Color options:** Gold, silver, black, red, blue, green, pink, rose gold, holographic, and matte finishes. Multiple colors can be combined in one design.

### Flip (Reversible) Sequin Patches

Flip sequins are the interactive option. Each sequin is double-sided with a different color on each face. When you brush your hand across the patch, the sequins flip to reveal the alternate color or a hidden design.

**Best for:** Kids' clothing, novelty merchandise, interactive branding, fashion accessories, and products that benefit from a "surprise" element.

**Color combinations:** Any two-color pairing. Popular combinations include silver/gold, black/red, blue/pink, and holographic/matte.

### Mermaid Sequin Patches

Mermaid sequins (also called two-way sequins) are a specific style of flip sequin where the sequins are slightly larger and create a smooth, wave-like transition between colors when brushed. The effect resembles mermaid scales, which is how the name originated. Mermaid sequin patches are popular for festival fashion, kids' apparel, and statement pieces on tote bags and backpacks.

### Sublimated Sequin Patches

A newer option where custom full-color designs are printed directly onto sequin surfaces using sublimation. This allows photo-realistic imagery on sequin patches, combining the sparkle of sequins with the detail of printed patches.

**Best for:** Photo-based designs, complex artwork, or brand imagery that requires more than 2-3 colors with a sequin sparkle effect.

## Design Tips for Custom Sequin Patches

Based on producing over 1,000,000 patches, here are our recommendations for sequin designs:

**Keep designs bold and simple.** Sequins are 3 to 6mm in diameter, so very fine detail, thin lines, and small text do not reproduce well. Bold letters, large logos, and simple graphic shapes create the strongest impact.

**Choose high-contrast color combinations for flip sequins.** The "reveal" effect works best when the two colors contrast strongly. Silver/gold, black/red, or holographic/matte combinations create the most dramatic brush effect. Low-contrast pairings (light blue/white) lose the interactive appeal.

**Consider the sparkle factor in your design.** Sequin patches look different in photographs than in person because of how sequins reflect light. Request a physical sample if the patch will be photographed frequently (product shots, social media, marketing materials).

**Plan for the application.** Sequin patches are not waterproof. They should not be applied to outdoor gear, rain jackets, or items that will be submerged. For water-resistant applications, choose [PVC patches](https://pandapatches.com/custom-patches/pvc) instead.

**Size matters for sequin patches.** Patches under 2 inches do not display the sequin effect well because there are too few sequins to create a cohesive shimmering surface. Sequin patches work best at 3 inches and above.

## Backing Options for Sequin Patches

**Sew-on backing (recommended for sequin):** The most secure attachment method. The seamstress stitches around the border of the patch for permanent placement. Included free.

**Iron-on backing:** Heat-activated adhesive applied with a heat press at 300 to 350 degrees. Works for most sequin patches on cotton and polyester blends. Use a pressing cloth between the iron and the sequins to avoid melting. Included free.

## How to Care for Sequin Patches

Sequin patches require more careful handling than embroidered or PVC patches:

- **Washing:** Turn garments inside out before washing. Use cold water on a gentle cycle. Avoid bleach. Hot water and harsh detergents can damage sequin coatings and loosen stitching
- **Drying:** Air dry only. Never put sequin-patched garments in a tumble dryer. The heat can warp sequins and melt adhesive backings
- **Ironing:** Never iron directly on sequins. If the garment needs pressing, turn inside out and iron the reverse side, or use a pressing cloth between the iron and the sequined area
- **Storage:** Store flat or hang. Do not fold garments with sequin patches, as the pressure can bend or crack sequins over time
- **Flip sequins:** Flip sequin patches may develop a "favorite" direction after repeated brushing. This is normal and does not affect the patch's function or appearance
