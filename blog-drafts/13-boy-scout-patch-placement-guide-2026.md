# Boy Scout and Cub Scout Uniform Patch Placement Guide

> **Key Takeaways**
> - Rank patches go on the left chest pocket, centered
> - The American flag patch goes on the right sleeve, below the shoulder seam
> - Council patches go on the right sleeve, at the top position closest to the shoulder
> - Merit badges belong on the merit badge sash, not on the uniform shirt
> - Order of the Arrow flap patch goes on the right pocket of the uniform shirt

For Scouts, every patch tells a story. From early achievements to higher ranks, patches represent dedication, skills, and milestones that shape the scouting journey. Understanding proper scout uniform patch placement is essential for displaying these accomplishments with pride. Whether you are part of the Boy Scouts, Cub Scouts, or advancing through the Order of the Arrow, correct placement reflects both respect for tradition and a sense of unity within the scouting community.

## Boy Scout Uniform Patch Placement

When it comes to boy scout uniform patch placement, the official guidelines are very specific. The left pocket, right pocket, sleeves, and shoulders all have designated areas for particular patches.

Rank patches are placed in the center of the left pocket. The American flag patch sits on the right sleeve directly below the shoulder seam. Leadership and position patches are positioned on the left sleeve, making achievements and responsibilities clear to the troop.

### Right Sleeve Stacking Order (Top to Bottom)

| Position | Patch |
|---|---|
| 1st (top) | American flag |
| 2nd | Council strip |
| 3rd | Troop number |
| 4th | Years of service star (if applicable) |

### Left Sleeve Stacking Order (Top to Bottom)

| Position | Patch |
|---|---|
| 1st (top) | Position of responsibility patch |
| 2nd | Patrol medallion |
| 3rd | Den chief cord (if applicable) |

## Cub Scout Uniform Patch Placement

Younger Scouts begin their journey with Cub Scouts, and proper cub scout uniform patch placement sets the foundation for future achievements.

| Patch | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pack number | Right sleeve | Below shoulder seam |
| Council patch | Right sleeve | Above pack number |
| Rank badge (Bobcat, Wolf, Bear, Webelos) | Left pocket, centered | Changes with each rank |
| Arrow of Light | Left pocket, below rank | Highest Cub Scout rank |
| Activity badges | Webelos colors worn on left sleeve | After earning |
| World crest | Left chest, above pocket | Same as Boy Scout uniform |
| Academics and sports pins | Worn on activity uniform | Not on field uniform |

Patches in Cub Scouts include rank advancements such as Bobcat, Wolf, Bear, and Webelos, as well as activity badges and elective achievements. This structure helps Cub Scouts proudly display their progress while learning about the importance of discipline and order.

## Order of the Arrow Patch Placement

For older Scouts, advancing into the Order of the Arrow (OA) is one of the most prestigious achievements. OA patches are placed according to official BSA guidelines and are recognized across councils.

| OA Item | Placement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| OA lodge flap | Right pocket flap | Replaces standard pocket flap |
| OA sash | Worn over right shoulder | On full dress occasions only |
| Vigil Honor patch | On OA sash | Highest OA honor |
| Brotherhood patch | On OA sash | Second level of membership |

From the Ordeal rank to Brotherhood and Vigil Honor, each patch placement carries deep meaning and represents a scout's ongoing commitment to service and leadership.

## Tips for Applying Patches Correctly

- Follow the official BSA Insignia Guide for the most current placement rules. Guides are available through the BSA website and local council offices.
- Measure before applying to ensure patches are centered and aligned properly.
- Use durable methods such as sewing, heat application, or strong adhesive backing for long lasting wear.
- For temporary placement, use a peel and stick adhesive sheet to test position before committing.
- Iron on application followed by a sewn border is the most reliable method for uniform patches that are washed frequently.
- Invest in custom patches for troop events, special occasions, or commemorative milestones.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Scout Patch Placement

**Where does the rank patch go on a Boy Scout uniform?**
The rank patch is centered on the left chest pocket of the Boy Scout shirt. It sits directly on the pocket itself, not on the pocket flap. As a Scout advances in rank, the new rank badge replaces the previous one.

**Where does the American flag patch go on a Scout uniform?**
The American flag patch goes on the right sleeve, at the top position closest to the shoulder seam. The star field faces forward, toward the front of the body.

**Do merit badges go on the uniform shirt?**
No. Merit badges are worn on the merit badge sash, not on the uniform shirt. The sash is worn over the right shoulder on formal occasions. Once the front of the sash is full, badges can continue on the back.

**What is the correct order of patches on the right sleeve?**
From top to bottom: American flag first, council strip second, troop number third. Any years of service stars go below the troop number.

**Where does the Order of the Arrow patch go?**
The OA lodge flap patch is worn on the right chest pocket flap of the uniform shirt. It replaces the standard pocket flap. The OA sash is worn separately over the right shoulder on formal OA occasions.

**Can Cub Scout patches be ironed on?**
Yes. Most Cub Scout uniform fabrics accept iron on patches at medium heat with a pressing cloth. For active scouts or frequently washed uniforms, sewing the border after ironing adds extra security. Always test on a small hidden area of the uniform before ironing near visible sections.
