## How We Ranked These Manufacturers

Every company was evaluated on six criteria:

**Pricing transparency:** does the supplier publish real prices on their website, or do they force you to request a quote? Suppliers that publish pricing make it possible to compare before you contact anyone.

**Verified review score:** Trustpilot or Reviews.io ratings only. Testimonials on a supplier's own website cannot be independently verified and are not counted.

**Turnaround time:** days from approved mockup to shipped order, for standard embroidered patches.

**Minimum order quantity:** the lowest number of pieces you can order at the base price.

**Setup and digitizing fees:** hidden charges that inflate the final price. Some suppliers bury these after you submit your design.

**Mockup and approval process:** does production begin only after you approve a digital proof, or does production start immediately after payment?

## What to Look for When Choosing a Custom Patch Manufacturer

Choosing between suppliers is not just about price. Here are the five questions that matter most before placing any order.

### 1. Does the supplier publish pricing?

If a supplier does not list prices on their website, you cannot compare them accurately and you have no benchmark before they quote you. Suppliers who hide pricing often add fees at checkout that were not mentioned in the initial quote. Setup fees, digitizing fees, art fees, and backing upgrade charges can add $40 to $150 to an order of 100 patches. Always request a complete written quote that itemizes every cost before committing.

For context on what patches should cost at various quantities, see our [full custom patch pricing guide](https://pandapatches.com/how-much-do-custom-patches-cost-full-pricing-breakdown).

### 2. What is the minimum order?

Most US suppliers require 25 to 100 pieces. If you need fewer than 50 patches, The/Studio (1 piece minimum) or Patches4Less (10 piece minimum) are the most flexible options. For [bulk custom patch orders](https://pandapatches.com/bulk-custom-patches) over 500 pieces, Panda Patches and Patches4Less offer the most competitive per-unit pricing.

### 3. Are the reviews independently verified?

Testimonials on a supplier's own website cannot be verified. Anyone can write or commission a positive testimonial. The only review platforms that authenticate customers before publishing reviews are Trustpilot, Google Reviews, and Reviews.io. Panda Patches is the only manufacturer on this list with a verified 4.8 Trustpilot rating from over 60 confirmed customers. Patches4Less holds the largest verified review count with 352 Trustpilot reviews at 4.3 stars.

### 4. Does production start only after your approval?

Some suppliers begin production immediately after payment. If your design needs changes after they start, you may be charged for a new run or denied a refund. Confirm in writing that production will not begin until after you explicitly approve a digital mockup. At Panda Patches, this is guaranteed on every order: nothing ships without your sign-off.

### 5. What does Velcro backing actually include?

For [military patches](https://pandapatches.com/custom-morale-patches), tactical gear, and uniforms, Velcro backing is standard. The critical question is whether the supplier includes both the hook side and the loop side in their Velcro price. Some suppliers quote for one side only and charge separately for the second. At Panda Patches, Velcro adds a flat $30 per order with both hook and loop sides included, regardless of quantity. For a deeper comparison of all backing options, see our guide to [custom Velcro patches](https://pandapatches.com/custom-velcro-patches).

### 6. What patch type do you actually need?

The most common mistake first-time buyers make is defaulting to embroidered patches when another type would serve them better. [Woven patches](https://pandapatches.com/custom-patches/woven) are better for designs with fine detail or small text. [PVC patches](https://pandapatches.com/custom-patches/pvc) are better for outdoor gear, tactical equipment, or anything exposed to water and heavy wear. [Chenille patches](https://pandapatches.com/custom-patches/chenille) are the right choice for varsity and letterman jackets. If you are unsure which type suits your design, read our [woven vs embroidered patches comparison](https://pandapatches.com/woven-vs-embroidered-patches) before ordering.
