## Types of Custom Tactical Patches

### Morale Patches

Morale patches are unofficial, often humorous or motivational patches worn to build unit camaraderie. They're the most popular tactical patch category and the one with the most creative freedom. Common themes include unit inside jokes, motivational quotes, cartoon mascots, skull designs, and pop culture references.

Morale patches are typically 2" x 3" or 3" x 2" with velcro backing for easy swap. PVC is the preferred material because it's waterproof, easy to clean, and holds bold colors and fine detail.

### Unit Insignia and Shoulder Sleeve Insignia (SSI)

Unit patches identify which military unit, police precinct, fire station, or security company the wearer belongs to. These are official patches that follow specific design standards and placement regulations.

For military unit patches, see our complete [OCP patch placement guide](https://pandapatches.com/tactical-and-military-ocp-patch-placement-options) for exact positioning on Army and Air Force uniforms.

According to [Army Regulation 670-1](https://armypubs.army.mil/epubs/DR_pubs/DR_a/ARN30302-AR_670-1-000-WEB-1.pdf), the Shoulder Sleeve Insignia is a required component of the OCP uniform that identifies the soldier's current unit assignment. The patch must be centered on the velcro-compatible area of the left shoulder.

**Unit patch specs:**
- Size: 2.5" to 3.5" (standard shoulder size)
- Material: Embroidered (traditional) or PVC (modern, weather-resistant)
- Backing: Velcro (standard for OCP uniforms)
- Colors: Subdued (OCP-compliant) or full color (dress uniforms)

## Design Tips for Tactical Patches

**Use subdued colors for field patches.** Per [Air Force Instruction 36-2903](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/104932/air-force-instruction-36-2903/), OCP-compliant patches use tan, brown, olive, and black. Avoid bright colors on patches intended for operational environments. Full color is reserved for garrison and dress occasions.

**Keep morale patches at 2" x 3" or 3" x 2".** This is the standard morale patch size that fits velcro panels on plate carriers, chest rigs, and tactical hats. Larger sizes may not fit standard velcro fields.

**Choose PVC for anything that sees water, mud, or heavy use.** PVC won't fade, fray, or absorb moisture. If the patch goes on a plate carrier, a rain jacket, or field gear, PVC is the right choice.

**Include a border for clean edges.** Merrowed borders (embroidered) or die-cut edges (PVC) give patches a finished, professional look. Borderless patches can look unfinished on tactical gear.

**Test readability at arm's length.** Tactical patches need to be readable at 3 to 5 feet for identification purposes. If text is too small to read at arm's length, increase the font size or reduce the word count.
