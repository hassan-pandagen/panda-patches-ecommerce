## What "No Minimum Order" Actually Means for Custom Patches

A no-minimum-order policy means you can order any quantity from 1 piece upward without meeting a minimum purchase threshold. In practice, most serious patch makers treat 5 to 10 pieces as the floor, because setup and thread-change costs are similar whether you stitch 1 patch or 10.

True no-minimum service means three things: no minimum quantity, no setup charge, and no rush penalty for small orders. Our own floor is 5 pieces on the embroidered side and 25 pieces on chenille. Below those quantities, machine calibration takes more time per piece than the finished patch is worth to the buyer.

According to the [IBISWorld 2025 custom apparel market report](https://www.ibisworld.com/united-states/industry/custom-apparel-embroidery/5242/), demand for short-run custom apparel and accessories has grown 8.4% annually since 2020 as small businesses and niche creators bypass bulk suppliers. Patch makers who serve this segment well must offer low minimums without compromising quality.

## Why Small Batches Cost More Per Piece (And How We Absorb Part of That Cost)

Custom patches have fixed production costs that scale poorly with quantity. Every order requires digitizing your artwork, calibrating the embroidery machine, mounting the thread cones, stitching a test patch, and quality-checking the final batch. That setup takes the same 30 to 90 minutes whether you order 5 or 500 patches.

At 1,000 pieces, the setup cost spreads across every patch and disappears into the per-piece price. At 5 pieces, the setup dominates the math. A vendor that charges the same $1.20 per piece at both quantities is either losing money on small orders or making it up elsewhere. Hidden fees, poor quality thread, or offshore production with 30-day lead times are the usual ways.

We absorb the setup cost directly into our small-batch pricing. You pay $180 for 50 pieces of standard embroidered patches ($3.60 each), not a $150 setup fee plus $1.50 per piece. Our pricing is always one number, no add-ons.

## What to Watch Out for With "No Minimum" Providers

Not every no-minimum claim is honest. The [FTC's 2024 rule on junk fees](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2024/12/ftc-announces-final-rule-banning-junk-ticket-hotel-fees) targets exactly this pattern: advertised prices that balloon once setup charges, art fees, and rush surcharges appear at checkout. Here is what to verify before placing an order.

**Setup fees disguised as "art fees" or "digitizing fees."** These are the same thing as a minimum, just split into a separate line item. Our digitizing is always free, first order and every reorder after.

**Quality dropoffs at small volumes.** Some vendors thin thread counts, substitute polyester backing for twill, or skip quality inspection for small orders. Ask for specs in writing.

**Extended lead times.** An 8-week lead time for 10 patches means the vendor is batching your order with their bulk production. Our 7 to 14 day lead time is the same whether you order 5 or 5,000.

**Offshore production only.** Overseas manufacturers often require 50-piece minimums in practice even if the website says "no minimum." We manufacture in the United States, which is why small batches ship in the same window as large ones.

**Per-piece rush fees on top of already-high small-batch pricing.** We publish a single rush fee per order (+$50 at 50 pieces, +$200 at 1,000 pieces), not stacked per-piece surcharges.
