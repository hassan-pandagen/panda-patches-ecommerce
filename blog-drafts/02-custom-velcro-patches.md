## What Are Velcro (Hook and Loop) Patches?

Velcro patches have two components: the **hook side** (rough, scratchy surface) sewn onto the back of the patch, and the **loop side** (soft, fuzzy surface) sewn onto the garment or gear. When pressed together, they grip firmly. When pulled apart, the patch detaches cleanly without damaging the fabric.

The term "Velcro" is actually a brand name owned by Velcro Companies. The generic term is "hook and loop fastener." In the patch industry, both terms are used interchangeably. Whether your order says "velcro backing" or "hook and loop backing," you receive the same product.

Custom velcro patches typically measure between 2 and 4 inches for shoulder and chest applications, though sizes up to 12 inches are available for back panels on tactical vests and jackets.

## Velcro vs Iron-On vs Sew-On: Which Backing to Choose

Choosing the right backing depends on how you plan to use the patch. Here is a side-by-side comparison:

- **Velcro (Hook and Loop):** Removable and reattachable. Best for uniforms, tactical gear, and any application where patches need to be swapped or removed for washing. Adds $30 per order. Both hook and loop sides included.

- **Iron-On:** Permanent attachment using heat-activated adhesive. Best for casual garments, jackets, and hats that are washed infrequently. Included free with every order. Applied with a household iron or heat press at 300-350 degrees for 15-20 seconds.

- **Sew-On:** Permanent attachment by stitching around the border. Strongest long-term hold. Best for garments that undergo heavy washing or physical stress. Included free with every order. Requires a sewing machine or hand stitching.

- **Sticker (Peel and Stick):** Temporary adhesive backing. Best for one-time events, trade shows, or temporary identification. Not recommended for items that will be washed. Included free with every order.

**When to choose velcro:** If your patches need to come off, velcro is the only option. If your patches are permanent, iron-on or sew-on will cost less (both are free) and provide a flatter profile against the garment.

## Tips for Designing Custom Velcro Patches

Based on producing over 1,000,000 custom patches, here are our top design recommendations for velcro patches:

- **Keep designs simple at small sizes.** Patches under 3 inches should use bold lines and limited text. Fine detail gets lost in embroidery at small scales.
- **Use high-contrast colors.** Dark backgrounds with light text (or vice versa) ensure readability from a distance, which is critical for identification patches on uniforms.
- **Choose PVC for outdoor use.** If your patches will be exposed to rain, sweat, or mud regularly, PVC rubber outperforms embroidery in durability and color retention.
- **Specify the loop panel placement.** Tell us exactly where the loop (soft) side will be sewn on your garment. This ensures the hook (scratchy) side is on the patch back, which is the standard orientation.
- **Order extra loop panels.** If you plan to attach patches to multiple garments, order additional loop panels. We include one set per patch by default.
