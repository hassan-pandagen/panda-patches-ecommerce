## What to Look for When Choosing a Custom Patch Manufacturer

### 1. Pricing Transparency
If a supplier does not publish pricing, you cannot compare them accurately. Suppliers who hide pricing often add fees at checkout — setup fees, digitizing fees, art fees. Always ask for a complete written quote with all fees included before committing.

### 2. Minimum Order Quantity
Most suppliers require 25 to 100 pieces. For small orders (under 50 pieces), The/Studio or PatchesByNumber offer the most flexibility. For bulk orders over 500 pieces, Panda Patches and Patches4Less offer the best per-unit pricing.

### 3. Verified Reviews
Testimonials on a supplier's own website cannot be verified. Look for Trustpilot, Google Reviews, or BBB ratings where reviewers are authenticated. Panda Patches is the only manufacturer on this list with a verified 4.8 Trustpilot rating from over 60 confirmed customers.

### 4. Free Mockup and Approval Process
Every supplier on this list offers a free digital mockup. The important question is whether production starts only after your approval. Some suppliers begin production immediately after payment — if you need changes, you pay again. Panda Patches holds production until you explicitly approve the mockup.

### 5. Backing Options
For military, tactical, and uniform patches, velcro backing is standard. Confirm your supplier includes both hook and loop sides in the velcro price. At Panda Patches, velcro adds a flat $30 per order regardless of quantity, with both sides included.
